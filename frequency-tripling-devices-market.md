<p><ul><li>Bare Crystal</li><li>Harmonic Module</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Frequency tripling devices are categorized mainly into two market types: bare crystals and harmonic modules. Bare crystals are standalone components that utilize non-linear optical processes to generate third harmonic frequencies from a fundamental wave. They are often used in specialized applications due to their simplicity and effectiveness. Harmonic modules, on the other hand, are more complex systems that integrate multiple components, including lasers and optics, designed for efficient frequency conversion. These modules offer enhanced performance and versatility for various advanced applications.</p></p>
