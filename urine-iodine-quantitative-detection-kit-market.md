<p><ul><li>Photoelectric Colorimetry</li><li>Catalytic Spectrophotometry</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Urine Iodine Quantitative Detection Kit market is primarily segmented into two types: Photoelectric Colorimetry and Catalytic Spectrophotometry. Photoelectric Colorimetry relies on the measurement of light absorbance to determine iodine concentration in urine samples, offering rapid and accurate results. In contrast, Catalytic Spectrophotometry involves the catalytic reaction of iodine with specific reagents, producing measurable changes in light absorbance. Both methods provide essential tools for assessing iodine deficiency and monitoring public health, making them vital in nutritional research and health assessments.</p></p>
