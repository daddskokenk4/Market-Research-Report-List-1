<p><p>Manual patch clamping is a laboratory technique used to measure the ionic currents flowing through individual ion channels in cells. This method involves using a glass pipette to create a tight seal with the cell membrane, allowing precise control of the environment around the cell and the ability to apply voltage or current stimuli. It's primarily utilized in neurobiology and cardiology research for studying cellular behavior and drug interactions.</p><p>The Manual Patch Clamp Market is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 4.02% during the forecast period, driven by increasing research activities in cellular physiology, pharmacology, and toxicology.
