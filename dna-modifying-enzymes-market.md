<p><p>The DNA modifying enzymes market includes various types essential for molecular biology applications. Alkaline phosphatases remove phosphate groups from nucleotides, preventing unwanted ligation reactions. Exonucleases digest DNA from the ends, allowing precise control over DNA fragments. Endonucleases, including restriction enzymes, cut DNA at specific sites for cloning and analysis. Terminal transferases add nucleotides to the ends of DNA molecules, facilitating modifications. Together, these enzymes are critical for research, diagnostics, and therapeutic applications in biotechnology and genetics.</p></p>
