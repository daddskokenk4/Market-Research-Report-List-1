<p><p>The Varicella Attenuated Live Vaccine market encompasses two main types: monovalent vaccines and combination vaccines. Monovalent vaccines are designed specifically to protect against varicella, the virus responsible for chickenpox, and are typically administered to children. Combination vaccines, on the other hand, integrate the varicella vaccine with other vaccines, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), providing broader immunization in a single shot. This dual approach enhances convenience, compliance, and overall public health outcomes by reducing the number of injections needed.</p></p>
